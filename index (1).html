The Process

Step-by-Step Galvanizing

1
Degreasing & Cleaning
Components cleaned in alkaline solution to remove oils, grease, and organic contaminants from steel surface.
2
Shot Blasting
High-velocity steel shot removes mill scale, rust, and oxides, creating a clean anchor profile for superior zinc adhesion.
3
Pickling (Acid Treatment)
Dilute hydrochloric acid removes remaining oxides and activates the steel surface for optimum flux treatment.
4
Flux Treatment
Zinc ammonium chloride flux prevents re-oxidation and improves zinc wetting during molten zinc immersion.
5
Hot-Dip Galvanizing @ 450°C
Immersed in molten zinc — zinc reacts metallurgically with steel forming bonded zinc-iron alloy layers with full coverage.
6
Quality Inspection
Coating thickness measured with magnetic gauge. Visual inspection for uniform coverage before dispatch.
Pre-Treatment
Shot Blasting
Before Galvanizing

Shot blasting before galvanizing removes surface contaminants like rust, mill scale, and old paint — ensuring a chemically clean surface for superior zinc adhesion. It creates a roughened profile that enhances mechanical bonding and ensures uniform coating throughout the component.

Removes Mill Scale & Rust
Eliminates all surface oxides and contaminants for chemically pure base metal.
Creates Anchor Profile
Roughened surface increases mechanical bonding area for stronger zinc adhesion.
Ensures Uniform Coating
Consistent profile results in even zinc thickness across the entire component.

Key Benefits

Why Galvanize?

50+ Year Corrosion Protection
Zinc coating provides 50+ years in rural, 25+ years in industrial environments — far exceeding paint systems.
Metallurgical Bond Strength
Zinc-iron alloy layers are harder than parent steel — cannot be peeled, chipped, or delaminated.
Zero Long-Term Maintenance
No re-painting or maintenance required for decades — dramatically reducing lifecycle costs.
Complete Surface Coverage
Penetrates internal surfaces, weld zones, recesses — areas inaccessible to brush or spray coatings.
Cathodic Protection
Zinc sacrifices itself to protect adjacent steel — even damaged areas remain protected by surrounding zinc.
Cost-Effective Long Term
Upfront galvanizing cost offset by eliminated maintenance, extended service life, and reduced replacement.
